## The Challenge

The Johnson family bought a beautiful 1920s Victorian home in Oakwood Estates, but soon discovered the low water pressure and rust-colored water were due to original galvanized steel pipes that were completely occluded with mineral buildup.

<Callout type="danger" title="Health Hazard">
Water testing revealed elevated lead levels from the solder joints, posing a serious health risk to the family. Immediate action was required.
</Callout>

## The Transformation

Preserving the original plaster walls and hardwood floors was the top priority. We chose a PEX repiping solution that allowed us to fish flexible piping through walls with minimal surgical cuts.

<Callout type="info" title="Preservation Focus">
We used surgical access points inside closets and cabinets to avoid cutting into the original crown molding and wallpaper.
